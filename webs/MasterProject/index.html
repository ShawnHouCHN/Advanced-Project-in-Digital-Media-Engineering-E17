Advanced Project in Digital Media Engineering


Does weather influences word choice on social media?

Intro

The project is about finding out whether the social media data can be used as a sensor to daily temperature changes. To contain the scope of the project, two regional data sources are introduced. One is the NOAA historic daily temperature data of USA and the other is the Twitter tweets given by a portion of users from USA. The timeline is constrained to 2016 at beginning. The dataset can be downloaded here



Data Preprocessing - NOAA

The temperature data given by NOAA is collected from meterological stations cross entire USA. The data is quantified, but since the project utilizes a county as unit in grid for analysis, each station has to be annotated for one of the county and then used to compute the mean daily temperature of the county. It turns out that some counties which are relatively small in area size and population do not possess any meterological station. To compensate missing data for those counties, KNN is used so that the countie takes into account of 8 closest daily station reads and computes an inverse-distance-weighted average temperature. Data Preprocessing - Twitter

The raw tweets data is processed by filtering through a manual dictionary which maps hot and cold-related terms to hot and cold count. Each tweet is then labelled to either "hot" or "cold" by evaluating the sum of hot and cold count. Regarding the source of a tweet, the probability densities are estimated by considering only tweets for which I have both GPS coordinates and a location text string. From the GPS coordinates, we can know exactly which counties the tweets have originated from. Normally, a major city in USA can cover many counties. The probability of where and which county the tweet is orignal from are then computed respectively. By using Maximum Likelihood Estimation, the county that the tweet is from can be determined.

Data Processing

The two datasets are standardized and integrated together for correlation evaluation. Some typical cities are selected as samples for illustration. The hot and cold count are also integrated into one scale to represent the strength of using the keywords.

Data Processing

WordCount Transformation

The two independent counts have been standardized and integrated into one variable which bounces between -1 and 1. The closer the value of the county is to 1, the more hot-related words used in the county and vice versa. The formula is shown to the right. H and C respectively denote vector of hot/cold count.

Correlation Distribution

To clearly illustrate the distribution of the correlation, the top 20% of counties with most amount of the tweets data are used to perform the correlation between the integrated strength and the daily temperature. The majority of the county has a Pearson value around 0.4 which indicates a strong correlation between daily temperature and word choice.

Typcial City

To see the trends of both temperature and word strength, a typcial metropolis of USA, Los Angeles, is selected for the same of line chart to the right. It is obvious that both lines are fluctuant, but in small ranges of time, the trends of two lines are clearly correlated. The rise of temperature does cause the word strength climbs up as well which indicate a certain increment of hot-related word usage on social media.
